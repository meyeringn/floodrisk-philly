How it works Three kinds of information, kept separate. 1 Official mapped hazard We query FEMA's flood map at the searched point and report the zone returned by FEMA. 2 Local limits and context We explain what the federal map may miss, including sewer backups, intense rain, and building-specific conditions. 3 Your renter plan You can add floor, exit, disability, power, and pet information to personalize preparedness guidance. Why this exists Flood tools rarely start with renters. Renters may not control drainage, building repairs, elevators, or emergency procedures, but they still face damaged belongings, displacement, missed work, and inaccessible exits. FloodRisk Philly turns a map result into practical questions and actions. Important limitation FloodRisk Philly provides educational information and renter preparedness guidance. It is not an insurance determination, engineering assessment, emergency warning, legal opinion, or guarantee that a property will or will not flood.
