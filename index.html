What the map may miss

Heavy rain and sewer flooding

FEMA maps focus mainly on river and coastal flooding. They do not fully capture basement backups, overwhelmed street drains, or water entering during intense rainfall.

      How it works

      Three kinds of information, kept separate.

      1

      Official mapped hazard

      We query FEMA's National Flood Hazard Layer at the searched point and report the zone returned.

      2

      Local limits and context

      We explain what the federal map may miss, including sewer backups, intense rain, and building-specific conditions.

      3

      Your renter plan

      You can add floor, exit, disability, power, medication, and animal information to personalize preparedness guidance.

      Why this exists

      Flood tools rarely start with renters.

      Renters may not control drainage, building repairs, elevators, or emergency procedures, but they still face damaged belongings, displacement, missed work, and inaccessible exits. FloodRisk Philly turns a map result into practical questions and actions.

      Works when the weather does not cooperate

      Install the preparedness guide for offline access.

      The app shell, planning questions, saved checklist, and official resource links can remain available after installation. A new address or FEMA lookup still requires an internet connection.

      Method and transparency

      What the app checks—and what it does not.

      Address

      Philadelphia's public address locator matches the entered street address and returns coordinates and ZIP code.

      Mapped flood hazard

      The app queries FEMA's National Flood Hazard Layer at that exact point and translates the returned zone into plain language.

      Personal plan

      The checklist is generated from answers about the unit, building access, disability, medical needs, and animals. It is guidance, not a formal risk score.

      App version: 9

      Important limitation

      FloodRisk Philly provides educational information and renter preparedness guidance. It is not an insurance determination, engineering assessment, emergency warning, legal opinion, or guarantee that a property will or will not flood.
