Purpose FloodRisk Philly is a plain-language educational tool for Philadelphia renters. It locates an address, reports the FEMA flood-zone classification returned for that point, explains the result's limits, and creates renter-focused preparedness guidance. Three kinds of information The tool keeps official mapped hazard, local context, and apartment-specific preparedness factors separate. It does not combine them into a proprietary risk score. • Official mapped hazard: the FEMA zone returned for the searched point. • Local context: information about hazards the federal map may not capture, such as sewer backups or intense rainfall. • Apartment factors: floor level, exits, elevator reliance, powered equipment, evacuation assistance, and pets. FEMA interpretation Higher-hazard areas generally include Special Flood Hazard Areas such as Zones A, AE, AH, AO, AR, A99, V, and VE. Moderate mapped hazard generally includes the 0.2-percent annual-chance flood area. Zone X and similar areas may represent lower mapped federal hazard, but lower mapped risk never means zero risk. If FEMA returns no conclusive feature or the service fails, FloodRisk Philly displays an unknown result. It never substitutes a lower-risk classification. Address lookup The site removes common apartment and unit identifiers, geocodes the street address, confirms the result is in Philadelphia, and displays the interpreted address back to the user. • Full street addresses are more precise than ZIP codes or neighborhood names. • A point lookup near a flood-zone boundary may be sensitive to geocoding accuracy. • Users should verify official boundaries through FEMA's Flood Map Service Center. Neighborhood and sewer indicators The original prototype included neighborhood sewer and overflow ratings. These should not appear as precise building-level findings until every value has a named source, update date, geographic unit, reproducible calculation, and documented treatment of missing data. Accessibility The map is supplementary. Every decision-relevant finding appears as text. The interface supports keyboard use, visible focus, live status announcements, reflow, reduced motion, forced colors, and English and Spanish content. Important limitation FloodRisk Philly is not an insurance determination, engineering assessment, emergency warning, legal opinion, or guarantee that a property will or will not flood.
